## Content Pipeline

```
IDEA
  ↓
DRAFT (markdown in content repo)
  ↓
REVIEW (git diff / PR)
  ↓
PUBLISH
  ├── Blog/Website → Sanity CMS → auto-rendered on site
  ├── Social Media → Copy from markdown → post manually
  ├── Digital Product → Export to PDF / Gumroad
  └── Newsletter → Kit (ConvertKit)
```

## Brand Voice Guidelines

### Before Maps
- **Tone:** Confident, unhurried, honest
- **POV:** First-person founder voice
- **Key phrases:** "before the mainstream," "personally scouted," "transparent pricing"
- **NOT:** Generic travel agency copy. Not exclusively about hidden/undiscovered places.

### HARP6X
- **Tone:** Technical, precise, systems-oriented
- **POV:** Builder sharing blueprints
- **Topics:** AI workflows, cybersecurity, creator systems, productivity

### ThereGoesUddip (TGU)
- **Tone:** Personal, vulnerable, reflective
- **POV:** First-person field journal
- **Topics:** Solo India travel, Himalayan treks, emotional essays, budget logistics

### Ms Paul Therapies
- **Tone:** Warm, professional, empathetic
- **POV:** Third-person professional (Aishani's voice)
- **Topics:** Mental health education, therapy demystification, coping strategies

## Cross-Pollination Rules

1. **Before Maps ↔ TGU:** Travel content can cross-promote. TGU personal essays can reference BM journeys.
2. **HARP6X ↔ TGU:** Keep separate. Different audiences, different voice.
3. **Aishani Content ↔ Ms Paul Therapies:** 1:1 mapping. All content serves the therapy practice.
4. **Before Maps ↔ Ms Paul Therapies:** No cross-promotion. Different brands, different audiences.
